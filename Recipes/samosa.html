Samosa

Description

Ingredients To Make Samosa

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water (to knead the dough)
  • 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled, peeled, and diced
  • 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder) or chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional)
  • Oil for frying

Steps To Make Samosa

  1. Prepare the dough by mixing all-purpose flour, salt, and oil/ghee in a bowl.
  2. Knead the mixture into a stiff dough using water as needed.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Boil, peel, and dice the potatoes. Cook the peas until tender.
  5. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  6. Saute chopped onions until golden brown.
  7. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, saut√© for a minute.
  8. Add diced potatoes, cooked peas, and spices (coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, amchur/chaat masala, and salt). Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  9. Optionally, add chopped fresh coriander leaves. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.
  10. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls and roll each ball into a thin oval-shaped disc.
  11. Cut each disc in half to form two semi-circles.
  12. Take one semi-circle, moisten the straight edge with water, and form a cone shape by overlapping the moistened edge over the dry edge.
  13. Fill the cone with the prepared potato-pea filling and seal the top using a fork or your fingers.
  14. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or skillet over medium heat.
  15. Fry the samosas until golden brown and crisp, then drain excess oil on paper towels and serve hot with chutney.
